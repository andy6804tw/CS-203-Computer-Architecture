Carry Look Ahead Adder Online help

How to Run
  • Two numbers are added in this program. The two numbers can be given in the boxes named as A and B.
  • Two numbers can be given in either bin (binary) or dec (decimal) number system.
  • Next, the total number of bits in the adder should be specified in Total Bits field.
  • In this adder, the total number of bits are divided into a number of groups. So, in the field Group Size, you should specify the number of bits you want to consider in each group.
  • After that, you have to specify a few delays in the Signal Delays section.
    • Delay to calculate Si once the Ci is available (Ci to Si).;
    • Delay to calculate Si once the Ai is available (Ai to Si).
    • delay of a single gate (Single Gate).
Format of Output
    After giving all the above mentioned inputs, when you click on the Compute button, in the right frame of the page, you can see a diagram of the carry look-ahead adder circuit and the timing data.
    • In the topmost diagram, a number of rectangular blocks are shown in cascade. Those are the blocks which can find out the sum of two bits and a carry-in.That block is shown below:



    • Here, in this program, two levels of carry-look-ahead generators have been used. So, just below each block, we show the Gi* and Pi* that are being generated from that block.
    • Below that, we show the first level carry look-ahead generator block followed by the second level carry look-ahead generator. From each of this carry-look-ahead generator, we put a path to the block one level top of that for delivering the carry-in.

    At the bottom, we show the time taken to generate eachi sum bit.
